When a Standby Generator Makes Sense

If outages affect work-from-home needs, medical equipment, sump pumps, refrigeration, or home comfort, a standby generator can keep essential circuits running. We help you decide what needs backup power and design the electrical scope around those priorities.

Generator Sizing in Plain Language

Generator size is based on the circuits you want to power and the starting loads of motors and HVAC equipment. We review your panel, identify essential circuits, and outline what your system can support without overloading.

Transfer Equipment: The Safety Requirement

Standby generators require transfer equipment that isolates your home from the utility supply during an outage. This keeps power flowing safely and prevents backfeed to the grid. We design the transfer setup as part of the electrical scope.

What’s Included (and What’s Not)

  • Electrical planning for essential circuits and backup loads.
  • Transfer switch or transfer panel wiring and integration.
  • Connections from the generator to the electrical system.
  • Testing and walkthrough of how the system operates.

Typically Not Included

  • Gas or propane piping unless specifically scoped.
  • Concrete pads, landscaping, or exterior construction.
  • Maintenance contracts outside the electrical install.

Our Installation Process

  1. Assess: Review your goals, essential loads, and panel capacity.
  2. Plan: Determine transfer equipment and generator placement.
  3. Install: Complete electrical connections and secure routing.
  4. Test: Verify safe operation and explain the controls.

Generator FAQs

What does a standby generator power?

It can power essential circuits or a broader selection depending on the system size and design.

Do I need a transfer switch?

Yes. Transfer equipment is required to isolate generator power from the utility supply safely.

Can you install a generator I already purchased?

We can review the equipment and determine if it’s compatible with your electrical system and scope.

Is fuel supply included in the electrical scope?

Fuel supply and plumbing are typically separate from electrical scope unless specifically included.

How is generator size determined?

We review essential loads, starting requirements, and usage priorities to recommend sizing.

Can I power HVAC systems with a generator?

Depending on size and starting loads, some HVAC systems can be included in the plan.

Will the generator test itself?

Many standby units have automatic test cycles. We review how to use the controls after installation.

Do you handle service upgrades for generator installs?

If your panel or service needs changes for safe generator integration, we can scope that work.

Where is the generator installed?

We review placement options outside the home with access, clearance, and routing in mind.

Can you install generator-ready subpanels?

Yes, we can configure dedicated circuits in a subpanel for backup power.
