When a Panel Upgrade Makes Sense

Your electrical panel is the control center for every circuit in your home or business. When it’s undersized or worn, it can cause nuisance trips, overheating, or limited capacity for new equipment. A panel upgrade gives you safe, dependable power and a clear path for future projects like EV charging, workshops, or additions.

Common Signs You Need an Upgrade

  • Breakers trip frequently or won’t reset reliably.
  • Lights dim or flicker when major appliances start.
  • You smell heat or see discoloration around breakers.
  • Your panel is out of space for new circuits.
  • You’re planning an EV charger, hot tub, or major remodel.

What’s Included (and What’s Not)

We focus on the electrical work itself—clear scope, clean wiring, and safe connections.

  • Load review to confirm capacity needs.
  • Panel replacement with properly labeled breakers.
  • Service corrections for unsafe or outdated wiring inside the panel.
  • Grounding and bonding checks for safety.

Typically Not Included

  • Drywall, paint, or carpentry repairs after electrical work.
  • Utility-side equipment changes outside the customer’s scope.
  • Upgrades to unrelated branch circuits unless requested.

Safety & Code Considerations

Panel upgrades are more than a swap—they require careful attention to conductor sizes, grounding, and breaker compatibility. We follow current electrical standards and manufacturer requirements to keep the system safe and dependable.

Our Panel Upgrade Process

  1. Assessment: We review your existing panel, circuit demand, and future plans.
  2. Planning: We outline the scope, routing, and breaker layout before work starts.
  3. Installation: We replace the panel, label circuits, and verify safe connections.
  4. Testing: We test circuits and walk through the changes with you.

Options to Consider

Many customers add whole-home surge protection during a panel upgrade. It’s optional, but it can help protect sensitive electronics and appliances from voltage spikes.

Panel Upgrade FAQs

Will my power be off during the upgrade?

Yes. The service must be shut down while the panel is replaced. We coordinate timing to minimize disruption.

Can I keep my existing breakers?

Most upgrades involve new breakers matched to the new panel for compatibility and safety.

Do I need a panel upgrade to add an EV charger?

Not always. We confirm available capacity first and recommend an upgrade only if needed.

How do you size a new panel?

We review current loads, planned additions, and the number of circuits to select an appropriate capacity.

Will you label every circuit?

Yes. Clear labeling helps you and future service calls understand what each breaker controls.

Can you address safety concerns inside the panel?

We correct unsafe terminations and organize conductors as part of the panel upgrade scope.

Can you add surge protection at the same time?

Yes, we can include whole-home surge protection as an optional add-on.
