Level 1 vs. Level 2 Charging

Level 1 charging uses a standard 120V outlet and is the slowest option. Level 2 charging uses a 240V circuit and charges much faster, making it the most practical choice for daily driving. We install Level 2 chargers with dedicated circuits sized to your equipment and wiring path.

What We Check Before Installation

  • Panel capacity: We confirm available amperage before adding a new circuit.
  • Routing: We plan the cleanest, safest path from panel to charger location.
  • Exposure: Indoor vs. outdoor placement affects conduit, weatherproofing, and hardware.
  • Breaker compatibility: We match breaker and wire sizes to your charger’s requirements.

EV Charger Installation Process

  1. Plan: Review charger location, electrical capacity, and routing options.
  2. Prepare: Install the dedicated circuit and safety components.
  3. Mount: Install the charger hardware and verify secure connections.
  4. Test: Confirm charging performance and review basic operation.

Common Scenarios We Handle

  • Detached garages requiring trenching or long conduit runs.
  • Multi-vehicle households needing load planning.
  • Business or fleet chargers in light commercial settings.
  • Homes that need a panel upgrade before adding a charger.

EV Charger FAQs

Do I need a dedicated circuit?

Yes. Level 2 chargers require a dedicated 240V circuit sized to the charger’s output.

Can you install a customer-supplied charger?

Yes, as long as it’s compatible with your electrical system and properly rated.

What if my panel is full?

We can review options such as load management or a panel upgrade before installation.

Is outdoor installation possible?

Yes. We use weather-rated equipment and conduit suited for exterior locations.

How long does installation take?

Most installations are completed in a single visit once the plan is confirmed.

Can you help me choose a charger size?

We can review your vehicle’s requirements and electrical capacity to guide the choice.

Do you install chargers for small businesses?

Yes, we handle light commercial installations and can plan for multiple chargers.
